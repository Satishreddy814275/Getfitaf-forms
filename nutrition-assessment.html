11 Golden Rules for a Healthy Diet

Your starting point for better nutrition. Focus first on the rules most relevant to your Yes answers above.

  1. Limit your intake of sugar, sweets, cakes, desserts, and pastries.
  2. Cut down on refined carbohydrates such as white bread and biscuits.
  3. Eat more beans, lentils, and whole grains — oats, brown rice, quinoa, rye, millet, or whole wheat.
  4. Include more dark leafy and root vegetables — broccoli, spinach, peppers, carrots, sweet potatoes, and green beans.
  5. Eat at least 3 portions of fresh fruit per day — apples, bananas, berries, melon, or citrus fruits.
  6. Drink a minimum of 8 glasses of sugar-free fluids daily — water, herbal teas, zero-calorie flavoured water, or fruit teas.
  7. Take a high-quality multivitamin and mineral supplement, 1,000mg of Vitamin C, and Omega 3 & 6 fatty acids every day.
  8. Avoid fried, burnt, or overly browned food, hydrogenated fats, and excess animal fat.
  9. Reduce your intake of processed meats and foods containing artificial additives, colours, and flavours.
  10. Avoid caffeine from coffee, tea, or energy drinks after 4pm.
  11. Avoid smoking and keep alcohol to one standard drink per day or less.
